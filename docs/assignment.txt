Project Details
The goal of this project is to develop an AI-supported system that can read and understand patent
documents, compare them with product designs, identify potential risks, and provide suggestions for
design improvement and innovation. The project is structured in four phases with accompanying
evaluation criteria.

Phase 1: Patent Understanding and Data Structuring
Collect and review patent documents (e.g., claims and descriptions). Extract key technical features
and organize them in a structured way. Build a simple database or schematic diagram for further
analysis.

Phase 2: Risk Identification
Compare product design inputs (e.g., key features or simplified descriptions) with patent content.
Identify overlapping areas and potential risks. Highlight critical features that may be sensitive.

Phase 3: Design Improvement Suggestions
Propose alternative design ideas to reduce or avoid risks. Ensure suggestions are realistic and
feasible from an engineering perspective. Provide clear and structured outputs.

Phase 4: Innovation Opportunities
Review multiple patents to identify common patterns and gaps. Suggest potential directions for new
ideas or improvements.

Evaluation Criteria / KPIs
• Accuracy of Information Extraction — How correctly key features are identified from patents.
• Risk Identification Quality — How well potential conflicts are detected.
• Quality of Suggestions — Usefulness and feasibility of design improvement ideas, evaluated
by experts.
• Innovation Relevance — Whether proposed ideas are meaningful and practical.
• Usability — Clarity and usefulness of the system from a user perspective.
